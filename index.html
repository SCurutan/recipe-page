Nonna Paola’s Fresh Pasta Dough

Story

Right after graduating from my first degree, I decided to do what all college kids do and travel Europe. And like all 22-year-olds, I didn’t have much money to my name, so I decided to take a small job overseas to pay for my trip of a lifetime. I was able to find one with a summer camp in Italy that boarded tutors and paid for their travel to the different regions of Italy. I was one of the lucky ones set to stay near Florence and I was the luckiest one as I was boarding with the very loving and hospitable Donati Family. One night, the nonna I was staying with, Paola, decided to teach me how to make fresh pasta. Strictly in Italian, I stumbled my way through the lesson and was able to make pappardelle successfully. Now back in Canada ten years later, I still remember that lesson like it was yesterday and hope that you too will make it through this recipe successfully – don’t worry if a couple of stumbling blocks get in your way.

Ingredients

  • 200 g “00” soft wheat flour
  • 200 g semolina flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp olive oil

Prep Time

20 min + 1 hour

Yield

4 servings

Rating

Reviews

Process

    STARTING

  1. Place flours on kitchen table and make a well in the center by using your hands to push flour away from the center while creating walls for the remainder of the ingredients. This should resemble a crater.
  2. Crack eggs into the center of the well.

    3. MIXING

  3. Using a fork, mix the eggs and the flour, incorporating a little at a time until everything is combined.

    4. KNEADING

  4. Knead the mixture together for about ten minutes. If the dough is too soft and stick, add flour and if it is too hard then add cold water a tablespoon at a time.

    5. RESTING

  5. After ten minutes, make a big ball and place a large bowl over the dough to prevent it from drying. Let it rest for 30 min at room temperature.

    6. ROLLING

  6. Divide dough into 4 pieces and knead each piece until smooth.
  7. Flatten the dough and dust with a little bit of flour. Start at the lowest setting on a pasta machine by passing the dough through the rollers. Once through, fold the dough and continue this process until the pasta is to the desired thickness.

    8. CUTTING

  8. Pass final rolled dough through the pasta cutters to desired pasta thickness.

    9. COOKING & STORING

  9. Boil salted water to cook the pasta for 30 s or leave at room temperature to dry pasta out to use at a later date.

Helpful Tips

  1. Use eggs at room temperature
  2. You can use water instead of eggs, but the dough will be less flavourful
  3. If you want to colour your dough, add vegetables or spices (e.g. spinach puree, herbs, beet juice) during the kneading process
  4. Thickness of pasta:
    • Tagliolini (1-2 mm)
    • Fettuccine (3-5 mm)
    • Tagliatelle (6-10 mm)
    • Pappardelle (10-30 mm)
